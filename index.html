Movement is Art.

The inspiration for this project came to me when I was on Pinterest and I saw photos of dancers in action. I started to imagine lights following the dancers' movements. The next morning I was in Photoshop creating this four-piece project.

I used a specialized brush in Photoshop and started to paint lines where and in a direction that I felt held the most power and movement. I wanted to have a different colour per picture to show that the emotions are different each time. The colours also depended on what music I was litening to at that moment.

I wanted the images to feel dynamic and inspiring to those who saw it. I want people to awe when they look at it.



I AM A SOLDIER

I have been a lover of SciFi novels ever since I could remember. I Am Number Four has always been dearest in my heart and a personal favourite despite the fact that they butchered the movie. I love Pittacu Lore's (the author) powerful statements he creates for the character's to say.

I had been thinking of some type of way to incorporate one of my favourite quotes with an image of the character who says it. It's too long of a quote to try an arrange around the face or below the neck but then I thought maybe I should put the words on the face...

A lot of failed tries, weird Photoshop outcomes, and erased sketches later, I had finally found the perfect way I wanted to create the image. I wanted to show that the quote was him, that it was the character and who he is and what he stands for.

Elizabeth - Toothless

A friend of mine approached me and asked if I would create a comission tattoo piece for him to honour his new, and first, baby girl.

He really wanted to incorporate a dragon and a dragon egg in the tattoo because of his passion for the mythical creature. We decided that to best represent both, the dragon would be coming out of the dragon egg. A baby dragon first hatching to represent his daughter being born. He loves Toothless from How to Train Your Dragon and asked if I could create a baby toothless.

What I ended up doing was making half an egg, cracked on top to show it had been broken through, and having the baby dragon's arms hang over the edge with its tongue out to show a little innocence and a cute factor. My client wanted the piece to be placed on his chest so I added one of the wings fully opened in order for it to be able to curve over the collarbone and on to the top to the shoulder.

Overall the client loved the piece and will be getting it inked very soon!

Shawn

Cityscape

I got the idea to create this project when I was in Photoshop messing around with photos of cities. I was adding text to the image and while I was changing the typeface I noticed the 3D button out of the corner of my eye. Wondering what the text would look like 3D I decided to click it. The way it was positioned made me think "if I put parts of the letters behind buildings it would look like they were actually in the city..."

After I had gotten a look I wanted, I decided I wanted to create an array of city images with their names integrated in the photo.
